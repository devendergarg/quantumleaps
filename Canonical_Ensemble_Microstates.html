About this Simulation
This simulation demonstrates why the Boltzmann distribution works by showing the full statistical story. The combined system (System + Reservoir) will be found in the configuration that has the maximum number of possible microstates (Ω).

1. The number of reservoir microstates, **Ω**, is too large to display directly. We show it in a special format: `Factor × 10^(Base)`. This separates the simple multiplicative `Factor` from the incomprehensibly large common `Base`.

2. This revealed `Factor` is the physical **Ratio of Microstates**. If the factor for State 1 is 8.00 and for State 2 is 1.00, it means the reservoir truly has 8 times more available configurations when the system is in State 1.

3. This physical ratio of available states (Ω₁ / Ω₂) is exactly what the mathematical Boltzmann Factor ratio describes, and it directly determines the final probability of finding the system in each state.
