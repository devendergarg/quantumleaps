2D Standing Wave Explorer

An interactive visualization of standing waves. Explore resonant modes on square and circular membranes. The particle density is proportional to the wave amplitude.

What are you seeing?

This animation models the behavior of standing waves on a two-dimensional surface, like a drumhead. Standing waves occur when waves interfere with their own reflections, creating stable patterns of vibration at specific resonant frequencies.

  • Nodes: The areas that remain still (zero amplitude).
  • Antinodes: The areas with the largest vibration (maximum amplitude).

How to Interact

Square Membrane: Use the Frequency slider to discover different resonant modes. The most stable patterns appear when the frequency value is near a specific resonant value.

Circular Membrane: Select modes directly. Angular Modes (l) control the number of nodal diameters (lines crossing the center). Radial Modes (n) control the number of nodal circles.

Use the View Angle Presets or drag the visualization with your mouse or finger to change the camera perspective.
