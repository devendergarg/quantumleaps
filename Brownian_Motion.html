Brownian motion is the random movement of particles suspended in a medium. This simulation models this phenomenon by giving each particle a random "kick" in a 3D direction at each time step.

This process is the foundation of diffusion. You can observe key physical concepts:

  • The Mean Squared Displacement (MSD) measures how far, on average, particles have moved from their starting point. It grows linearly with time in an infinite system.
  • The Diffusion Coefficient (D) is calculated from the MSD and represents how quickly the particles spread out.
  • The Pooled Distribution plot shows how the particles' positions form a Gaussian (bell curve) distribution, a hallmark of random processes.
  • Using the Reflective Box, you can observe how a confined system reaches equilibrium, where the particles are uniformly distributed and the MSD stops growing.
